Geotechnical centrifuges come in two main flavours — **beam centrifuges**, which swing a model container at the end of a rotating arm, and **drum centrifuges**, where the model is built inside a spinning drum. Capacity is usually quoted in **g-tonnes** (payload × maximum acceleration).

I have been fortunate to work with several facilities directly — the HKUST centrifuge during my Ph.D. research, and the TU Delft and Deltares laboratories during the MIDAS and MIDASclay programmes. This page will grow into a practical guide to the world's major facilities, including:

* What each machine is (beam/drum, radius, g-tonne capacity)
* Specialist capabilities — earthquake shakers, in-flight robots, 4-DOF actuators, in-flight CPT
* What kinds of problems each laboratory specialises in
* How to get access — many facilities offer transnational access through programmes such as GEOLAB
